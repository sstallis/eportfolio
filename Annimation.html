Animation

I struggled initially to think of a quick topic for the 15-second timeframe. I ended up choosing the moon phases after my daughter was very excited to see the moon last night plus I used to be an earth science teacher. Fun fact I also was a contestant on the game show Let's Make a Deal and Wayne Brady quizzed me on the current moon phase (I did not get zonked!)

I used Adobe Animate to create my project. I have used Adobe projects before and thought I would pick up the program quickly but it took me some time to catch my bearings. I could use a ton more time playing around to be able to dig deep. I went through many iterations on how I wanted to create my moon phases. I started with pictures of the moon and tried to mask over the shadow later with a transparency on and it was not working how I envisioned. So then I want a simple route that aligns more with this project by creating all my moon objects and shadows as a vector. I used a simple black background and a black shadow that overlaid a grey moon. I would use a keyframe to move the shadow one frame at a time to make the shadow appear smooth across the surface of the moon. The onion tool was helpful to move the shadow just a little bit every time. Using the left and right keys to move over more precisely was also helpful. I added the name of each phase of the moon in a text keyframe. After all the phases and text were created I placed some start vectors around the background to give a space vibe. I slightly rotated the stars back and forth using keyframes to make the overall image fun and visually appealing as the phases were occurring. The moon starts in a new moon phase (not visible) goes through all the phases and turns back to invisible to start the cycle over again.

My narration was not surer informative as there are so many phases and only a short amount of time so I ended up announcing each phase as they occurred. I also added a little space theme audio to the background to create a fun atmosphere for the project (space joke).
